PanView

Privacy Policy

Short version: PanView does not collect, store, or sell your personal data. The app uses your location only to show you nearby species. No account is required.

Effective Date

This policy is effective as of March 2026 and applies to the PanView mobile application.

Information We Do Not Collect

PanView does not require you to create an account. We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personally identifying information.

Location Data

PanView requests access to your device's GPS location to display species observed near you. Your location is used only in the moment — it is not stored on our servers, not logged, and not shared with any third party by PanView.

You may deny location permission at any time through your device settings. Without location access, the app's core features will not function.

Camera

PanView includes an optional photo identification feature. When you use it, your photo is sent to the iNaturalist API for species identification. PanView does not retain or store photos. Images are processed by iNaturalist under their own privacy policy, available at inaturalist.org/pages/privacy.

Third-Party Services

PanView relies on the following third-party services to deliver its features:

  • iNaturalist — species observation data and photo identification. Your GPS coordinates are sent to iNaturalist to retrieve nearby observations. iNaturalist's privacy policy applies to data processed by their API.
  • Supabase — hosts PanView's species database. Queries sent to Supabase contain only a state abbreviation derived from your location. No personal data is transmitted.
  • Mapbox — provides the map layer. Mapbox may process map tile requests in accordance with their privacy policy.
  • Wikipedia — species descriptions are fetched from the Wikipedia API. No user data is sent to Wikipedia.

Data Retention

PanView does not maintain any database of user activity, location history, or usage logs. Each app session is independent and no data persists on PanView's systems between sessions.

Children's Privacy

PanView does not knowingly collect data from children under the age of 13. The app is designed to be used by people of all ages as a nature awareness tool, but it does not collect personal information from any user regardless of age.

Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy changes materially, we will update the effective date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact

Questions about this privacy policy can be directed to the PanView developer via the contact information listed on the App Store or Google Play listing.
